## Key ideas

1. **Four Approaches to AI Implementation**
   - The article examines the main paradigms for bringing AI solutions to market
   - Each approach has distinct strengths and weaknesses
   - The choice significantly impacts development complexity and deployment success

2. **Agent-Based Approach**
   - Likely involves autonomous systems that can make decisions and take actions
   - May offer flexibility but potentially at the cost of predictability and control
   - Requires sophisticated planning and reasoning capabilities

3. **Workflow-Based Approach**
   - Probably focuses on structured, predictable sequences of operations
   - Likely offers more control and predictability than agent-based systems
   - May be more suitable for well-defined business processes

4. **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) Approach**
   - Combines information retrieval with generative AI models
   - Likely excels at knowledge-intensive tasks requiring factual accuracy
   - Enables AI systems to ground responses in specific knowledge bases

5. **Skills-Based Approach**
   - Possibly refers to modular, composable AI functions
   - May offer balance between flexibility and control
   - Could relate to the skill system we just implemented in our knowledge-digest workflow
